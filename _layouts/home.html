Who we are

REdy is a pan-African online community. We unite millions of people in one influential network that assists and promotes local African interests at home and overseas.

What We Do

REdy was founded by Dr. Susann Dattenberg-Doyle in 1999 with the construction of two schools in Gbi Kpoeta, Ghana. Since that time, we have continued to promote education in Africa, publishing learning materials to an ever-growing community of more than 6.5 million people in over 45 countries.

Using our unparalleled insight into the lives and needs of people across Africa from local to continental trends, we create productive partnerships with organizations that share our passion and commitment to Africa.

Methodology

To ensure the best engagement with our community and Africa at large, REdy uses a clear chain of information to gather data.

Our social media platform, Right for Education, offers much-needed, free educational materials to people all over Africa, from major cities to rural communities. In partnership with Facebook’s Free Basics initiative, we can provide these materials free of data charges, meaning there are no restrictions to the volume of content our community can engage with.

As our community engages with our material, we gather their feedback. Our library has created a digital forum for a continent, where people meet to discuss their ideas, wants and needs. Thanks to this dialogue, we have unrivalled understanding of the impact of social, cultural, political, technological and economic changes across Africa. This information is the key to a prosperous future.
